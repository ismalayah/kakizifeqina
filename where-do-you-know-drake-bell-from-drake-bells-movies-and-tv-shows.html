    Despite only being in his mid-thirties, Bell has been working for nearly three decades now, and as a result, he has an acting resume that rivals almost anyone in showbiz in terms of length. If you’ve been following the news about the former child actor and are wondering where you know him from, you’re not alone. While he’s certainly best known for his Nickelodeon roles, there is a chance you may recognize him from one of his more obscure projects.

    Who is Drake Bell?

    Bell is a former Nickelodeon star and child actor who has appeared in numerous films and television shows. Born in California, he began acting in commercials at age five, and as a child, played several small roles in some iconic television shows like Seinfeld and Home Improvement. Bell’s big break came in 1999 when he earned a starring role on the comedy sketch program The Amanda Show. Two years later, he starred alongside Josh Nichols in Drake and Josh, which turned him into a household name and earned him several awards.

    Bell is also a musician and has released five studio albums under his own name, as well as multiple singles. His sixth studio album, Non-Stop Flight, is set to come out later this year. However, his musical career hasn’t quite reached the heights of his acting, with only one of his studio albums charting in the top 100 in the U.S (It’s Only Time). With that said, he’s had two number ones in Mexico, as well as another Top 10 single in Central America.

    Since 2009, Bell has been involved with the Thirst Project, a charitable organization that seeks to raise awareness about a lack of clean water. The Drake and Josh star has raised money via concerts and appeals. He has also engaged in various other charitable projects.

    Bell’s life hasn’t been without controversy, either, with financial woes and accusations of abusive behaviour. He also worked closely with Dan Schneider at Nickelodeon, who has been accused by multiple former child actors of coercive and abusive behaviour. Bell has not commented publicly on Schneider.
