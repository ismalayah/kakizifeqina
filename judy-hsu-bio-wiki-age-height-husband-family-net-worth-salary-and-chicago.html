    Who is Judy Hsu?, Judy Hsu Biography and Wiki

    Judy Hsu is an Emmy award-winning anchor who joins ABC7’s primary daytime lineup after 15 years of “waking up” with Chicago, she was born in Taipei, Taiwan, Judy remains fluent in Mandarin and brings Chicago news a global perspective. Judy now anchors the top-rated 4 PM and the 6 PM News at the #1 news station in Chicago. Additionally, she hosts a long-format interview segment that airs on Sunday mornings called “Newsviews”, offering unique perspectives from Chicago area newsmakers as they address local as well as national topics pertinent to our community.

    She also serves as the primary host of ABC7’s signature half-hour program Asian Influences, which highlights extraordinary contributions by Chicagoans of Asian Pacific American descent, while examining issues impacting the community.

    Judy was thrilled to return to her hometown in the year 2001 to join ABC 7 as the weekday anchor of the station’s morning 4:30-7 AM block of news programming. Judy helped lead the morning show for 15 years before her promotion to the afternoon and early evening.

    Judy’s roots in the Windy City takes her back to elementary school, Judy’s family immigrated to Chicago from Taiwan when she was 11 years old. From living in Chicago’s Chinatown to Rogers Park to Morton Grove to later attending the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign as well as then landing an on-air reporting job the day after graduation, she truly embodies the spirit of the American Dream success story.

    During Judy’s tenure at ABC 7, she has been a part of just about every major story in Chicago either reporting in the neighborhoods or anchoring from the news desk. Judy also reported live from the White House bringing ABC7 viewers extensive coverage of her one-on-one interview with President Barack Obama just before he left the Oval Office. While a rookie reporter in California, Judy covered the O.J. Simpson verdict live from the L.A. courthouse and Judy was one of the youngest reporters to fly with the Blue Angels!

    Judy’s on-air work has garnered multiple Emmys including awards for breaking news coverage as well as for her work on ABC 7’s programming specials Asian Influences as well as the Magnificent Mile Lights Festival. To date, she has received nearly two dozen Emmy nominations including “Outstanding Performance” as a news anchor.

    Before joining ABC 7, Judy anchored the 4 PM weekday newscast at KFMB-TV in San Diego, CA. Judy served as KFMB’s weekend anchor and general assignment reporter prior.

    Since coming back to Chicago, she has worked with dozens of charities throughout the region highlighting issues important to local families. She chairs the Advisory Board for Chicago’s Chinese American Service League and has been a leading voice in American Cancer Society’s annual Making Strides Against Breast Cancer Walk as well as the American Heart Association’s Go Red for Women movement. Judy was also honored to join the Community Memorial Foundation’s Regional Health and Human Services Advisory Council in the western suburbs and was named a Woman of Distinction in the year 2017.

    Judy received her B.A. in Broadcast Journalism from the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign, and began her broadcasting career at the campus radio station, WPGU-FM. Following an internship with legendary Chicago anchor Walter Jacobson, she landed her first TV job with WCIA in Champaign. One of her passions today is speaking to schoolchildren about the importance of pursuing their dreams.

    Speaking of kids, even while on maternity leave, she continued to bring us “breaking news”! Judy became the subject of national headlines for the “expressway baby” story when her fourth child was born on Chicago’s Eisenhower Expressway.

    Judy resides in the western suburbs of Chicago with her husband along with four children.
